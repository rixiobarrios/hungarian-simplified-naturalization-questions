# Hungarian Simplified Naturalization Questions Application

## Target Audience: Spouses of Hungarian citizens (5-10 years married).
## Focus: Conversational Hungarian (often around B1-B2 level required for a smooth interview).
## Key Content: Self-introduction, family, daily life, and basic Hungarian history/culture (e.g., March 15th, National Anthem).
## Goal: To help you avoid having to re-schedule due to insufficient language skills.

The Hungarian simplified citizenship process through marriage allows foreign spouses to acquire citizenship after 10 years of marriage (5 years if children are born) without a constitutional exam, requiring only basic Hungarian language skills and documentation. The process involves submitting proof of marriage, identity documents, and a personal, in-person interview to verify language proficiency. 

## Key Requirements & Process
Marriage Duration: At least 10 years of marriage, or 5 years if you have children together.
Language Proficiency: You must be able to communicate in Hungarian on a basic level during an interview with a consul or official.
Documentation: Birth certificate, marriage certificate (must be registered in Hungary), and proof of the spouse's Hungarian citizenship.
Interview: A short talk regarding why you want to become a citizen and your connection to Hungary.
Address: A Hungarian address card is not strictly required for this specific process. 

## Common Interview Questions (in Hungarian)
The interview focuses on confirming your identity, your relationship, and your ability to speak the language. 

Personal Information: 
What is your name? Where were you born? 
What is your occupation?

Relationship: 
How long have you been married? Where did you meet your spouse?

Connection to Hungary: 
Why do you want to be a Hungarian citizen? 
Have you visited Hungary? 
Do you speak Hungarian at home?

Language Test: 
The official may ask simple questions to ensure you can function in Hungarian, often related to your application documents.
